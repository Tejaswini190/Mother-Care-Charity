Blood Donation

Every drop counts. Blood donation is one of the most selfless acts that can save lives. At Mother Care Charity, we organize regular blood donation camps to help patients in need of immediate transfusions. One unit of blood can save up to three lives.

Our Blood Donation Camps

Blood donation camps are often explained through ideas from public health and social behavior. From a health perspective, they follow the voluntary non-remunerated blood donation model, promoted by the World Health Organization, which stresses that safe blood is best supplied by people who give freely, without payment, in a well-organized system.

Emergency Support

Emergency support in charitable and humanitarian work is often guided by a mix of disaster management and social-behavior theories. From a disaster management perspective, the Relief Recovery Continuum explains that assistance should begin with fast, life-saving measures (food, shelter, medical care) and then shift toward rebuilding and resilience once basic needs are met. Impact of Blood Donation

The impact of blood donation can be understood through several public health and social theories. From a public health perspective, the Health Belief Model explains that people are more likely to donate blood — and encourage others to do so — when they understand the benefits (saving lives, supporting surgeries or trauma care) and believe their effort has real value compared with any perceived barriers like fear or inconvenience.
